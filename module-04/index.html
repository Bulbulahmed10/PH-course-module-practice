Les Arcs La Plagne, France

TThe famous ski resort of Les Arcs is located in the heart of the French Alps, in the Haute-Tarentaise valley. Together with Peisey-Vallandry and La Plagne, it forms a single ski area called Paradiski. Les Arcs La Plagne includes four mountain stations, named after the altitude and having a common network of elevators — Arcs Pierre Blanche (1600 m), Arcs-1800, Arcs-1950, and Arcs-2000. The resort also includes the town of Bourg-Saint-Maurice, which lies at the foot of the mountain. The ski resort offers 425 kilometers of trails of varying difficulty levels, located at an altitude of 1200 to 3225 m. Skiing here will please both beginners and experienced skiers. The beginners usually choose the Arc-1800 zone, and the professionals greatly appreciate the Launched Kilometer speedway on the slope of the Aiguille Rouge. Each level has at least one downhill lit up at night. Les Arcs offers plenty of activity options for non-skiers as well. The Arc-1800 area has a snowpark with shapes, halfpipe, and border-cross trails. Several slopes are suitable for freeride skiing. Launched Kilometer trail can be used for ziplining. Dog sledding is available in the Arc 2000 area. Chambéry Airport is 127 km from Les Arcs, 145 km from Lyon, and 156 km from Geneva. Travel can be by shuttle, cab, or by bus. There are direct trains from Paris, Lille, Amsterdam, Brussels, and London to Bourg-Saint-Maurice, which is 15 km from Les Arcs.

Disneyland Resort — Anaheim, California, USA

Disneyland Resort in Anaheim is the realization of Walt Disney's dream of the perfect vacation park, a magical world of cartoons and fairy tales where wonders await at every turn. When it opened on July 17, 1955, the "king of animation" could answer precisely the many letters from children asking where the characters from their favorite stories lived. Mickey Mouse, Minnie, Snow White, the gnomes, Peter Pan, and Winnie the Pooh all settled in Disneyland. At the time of its opening, Disneyland Resort consisted of just one theme park, which, over time, has evolved into a grand resort complex. Now, on 200 hectares of land, there are two theme parks (Disneyland Park and Disney California Adventure), the Downtown Disney shopping complex with a 12-screen movie theater, three hotels, a lot of stores and restaurants.

The best things to do in Frankfurt, Germany

Römerberg is Frankfurt am Main's main square and the busiest pedestrian area. It represents the center of the old city - the Altstadt. The town hall has been located in the Römer complex, consisting of eleven buildings, since 1405. In front of it, you can see the Fountain of Justice (Gerechtigkeitsbrunnen), built in Renaissance style in 1543, and opposite stands the Old Church of St. Nicholas (Alte Nikolaikirche). Römerberg square is surrounded by old half-timbered houses, many of which contain restaurants, bars, and stores. In winter, a traditional Christmas market is held here.

EasyJet airline launches flight to observe northern lights

British airline EasyJet and charity foundation Aerobility are launching flights allowing one to admire the unique natural phenomenon — northern lights at an altitude of over 9,000 meters. These organizations already have cooperation experience. Eight years before the Covid-19 pandemic, hundreds of passengers had an opportunity to enjoy the magnificent sight from the sky. Some have also used it for romantic purposes — in February 2020, an Oxfordshire couple in love got engaged during the flight. The trip includes lunch at the Sofitel London Gatwick Hotel, a pre-flight briefing, informational accompaniment by northern lights experts from the British Antarctic Survey, and drinks and entertainment on board. The flight will depart from London's Gatwick Airport. Tourists will go to 61 degrees north latitude in search of a spectacular spectacle. You can book tickets for the flight on Aerobility's official website. Discounts are available for passengers with disabilities, their companions, and children under 16. Meals during the trip are included in the price. The proceeds from ticket sales will go to support people with disabilities.

The Royal Palace (Det kongelige slott)

The Royal Palace is the official residence of the Norwegian rulers. It is located on Bellevue Hill at the end of Karl Johans Street. Its construction began in 1824 and lasted until 1849. King Harald V and Queen Sonja reside in Det kongelige slott. Here they hold audiences and dinner parties, receive foreign heads of state, and convene the Council of State. In summer, the Royal Palace is open to the public.

Mount Kanlaon

anlaon is the highest mountain in the Visayas and one of the most beautiful active stratovolcanoes in the country. Only trained mountaineers can climb to the top and see the impressive crescent-shaped crater. The ascent along the steep mountain paths can take up to three days. But the difficulty of the route is compensated for by its scenic beauty. You have to walk through dense jungle, past waterfalls, hot springs, and caves. There are many pyroclastic cones and extinct craters on the mountain's slopes. The fact that Canlaon is an active volcano adds to the thrill for trekkers. But hikers are not allowed to approach the active Lugud Crater, which has been smoky since 2015. They go up to the base camp at the top, which offers a circular panorama of the Visayas

Mount Apo

Mount Apo is the highest mountain in the Philippines. It is located between North Cotabato and Davao and is the highest point on the island of Mindanao. Conquering the "foremother" of all Philippine mountains, as it is called here, is not easy, even for experienced climbers. Daredevils, who challenge and reach the summit, have a fascinating, yet daunting journey. The braves pass through dense jungle, mossy forests, green meadows, and mossy bogs, overcome a stormy river, and conquer sheer cliffs. They can see Mount Apo's four famous lakes along the way — boiling mud-filled Agco, crystal-clear Venado, turquoise Macadac, and Jordan. But the ultimate reward awaits at the top. Mount Apo rises 2,956 meters. Its summit offers an amazing view of almost the entire island of Mindanao, its majestic mountain ranges, and the endless sea. You will not find such stunning scenery anywhere else in the Philippines.

Nasher Sculpture Center

The Nasher Sculpture Center is one of Dallas' most popular museums, opened in 2003. It houses a collection of sculptures assembled by Raymond and Patsy Nasher. Exhibits include works by Picasso, Rodin, Matisse, Giacometti, Serra, and other distinguished masters. In addition to the permanent exhibit, the Nasher Sculpture Center hosts temporary displays and themed events. Sculptures are installed not only indoors but also outdoors.
